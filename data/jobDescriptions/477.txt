Northrop Grumman is seeking qualified candidates for a Senior Tax Manager, Special Projects, in Falls Church, VA. The qualified candidate will participate in the management of all aspects of the Company’s tax planning, compliance; and accounting for income taxes. The successful candidate will work with businesses, external auditors and other tax professionals in carrying out his or her responsibilities and participate and/or oversee the international compliance and reporting processes. Other duties include review of integrated business plans, cash tax forecasts, forward pricing analysis and special project assistance as needed by department leaders.

Responsibilities include, but are not limited to the following:

•Responsible for international tax compliance including the repatriation of non-U.S. earnings and preparation of required filings (5471s,8858,1118, 8992 and 8993)
•Calculation and reporting of non-U.S. tax provisions including process improvements
•Assist in review of the quarterly tax provision, including all work papers and evidence developed, in support of the quarterly and annual SEC filings
•Identify, quantify and document uncertain tax positions as required under ASC 740
•Review quarterly tax entries, including the annual provision to return reconciliation
•Liaison with business sectors on tax PBCs and tax accounting issues
•Maintain documentation for SOX and internal audit purposes
•Assist with various tax advisory projects
•Assist with integrated business plan, cash tax forecasts and forward pricing analysis
•Ensure all new tax guidance (FASB, SEC, Federal/State/International tax laws) are incorporated into the quarterly provisions and compliance filings
•Provide formal responses to inquires or other information requested by senior management, internal and external auditors

In this role, the selected individual will also have some responsibility for the following:

•Identify planning opportunities related to federal, state and international taxes (e.g. FDII, research and development, state apportionment, credit and incentives, etc.)
•Identify opportunities related to state credits and incentives and working with the corporate state tax planning team to secure them
•Review contracts and evaluate tax requirements and implications
•Assist the Corporate Tax on transaction support related to sector M&A activity
•Participate in sector operational meetings to identify tax opportunities and risks inherent in sector operations
•Provide tax support to the sector CFO


Qualifications

Basic Qualifications:
•Bachelor's degree in Finance, Accounting or related field
•CPA certification plus 12 years of progressive relevant tax experience in a public accounting or large corporate environment
• Strong experience with corporate tax provisions (ASC 740)
•Strong technical skills and experience of US GAAP and SEC reporting guidance
•Experience in translating local GAAP outside the USA into US GAAP
•Must have a demonstrated hands-on approach and success in working in a team-based environment
• Proven financial, analytical, technical and interpersonal skills
•Demonstrated ability to manage large projects, as well as prioritize and supervise/motivate staff
•Expected travel of up to 25%


Preferred Qualifications:
•Excellent client service and demonstrated personal development skills; motivated to grow in position and leadership role within the company
•Excellent written and verbal communication skills
•Familiarity with ERP systems and Hyperion
•Advanced skills in the use of SAP, HFM, MS Excel and CorpTax are strongly preferred
